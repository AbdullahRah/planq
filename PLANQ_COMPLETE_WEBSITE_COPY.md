## 4. What Planq Actually Does

### Reads the drawings.
Floor plans, elevations, schedules, sections. PDF or image. Multilingual labels — if your sheet is in Spanish or French, Planq still pulls the dimensions. Multi-page PDFs are rasterized and analyzed per page, up to 12 pages per upload. DXF files are parsed entity-by-entity: every LINE, DIMENSION, TEXT, and MTEXT pulled directly from the CAD source.

### Knows the code.
The 2020 NBC Alberta Edition is loaded in full — 1,953 indexed sections covering the 1,489-page document. Section-aware chunking splits by article number so every retrieval lands on a real, citable clause. Chunks are tagged by category — egress, doors, corridors, stairs, occupancy, fire — for faster, targeted retrieval.

### Cites the section.
Every flagged item links to the actual clause: §9.9.6.4 — Width of Exit Doors. No vague "looks off" comments. Every citation is verifiable.

### Catches Part-9 vs Part-3 confusion.
Knows that an 850 mm door is fine for a single-detached dwelling but undersized for a Group A assembly. Routes Part 9 (Housing & Small Buildings) plans to Part 9 thresholds and Part 3 plans to Part 3 thresholds. No more false positives from applying the wrong section to the wrong building type. When occupancy is unstated on a plan, Planq says so in the violation rather than guessing.

### Cross-checks every sheet.
Flags when door D-01 is 900 mm on A-1 and 850 mm on the door schedule. Flags when a stair shows up on A-2 but is missing from A-1. Catches dimension contradictions, missing elements, door width disagreements between floor plan and schedule, and occupancy metadata mismatches. Runs automatically when you upload two or more sheets.

### Grades the severity.
Three buckets: critical, major, minor. Critical means it won't pass review. Minor means clean it up at the next revision. So you know what to fix before submission and what can wait.

## 6. How It Works

### 1. Upload.
Drop your PDFs, scans, or DXFs into Planq. One sheet or twenty. File type is detected automatically. Drag-and-drop, multi-file — upload a whole package in one go.

### 2. Review.
Planq extracts the rooms, doors, corridors, stairs, dimensions, and occupancy classification from every sheet. Then it compares everything against the NBC — running five separate retrieval categories per sheet: egress, doors, corridors, stairs, occupancy — and against itself across all sheets. Watch each step land in real time: parsing, extracting, retrieving, analyzing.

### 3. Fix.
You get a graded list of violations with section numbers and an extracted plan data panel showing what was actually pulled off your sheets — so you can verify nothing got misread. Filter by severity. Filter by type. Know exactly what to fix, where, and why.

## 12. FAQ

**Does this replace a real plan review?**
No. It's a self-review tool. The municipality still has the final word. Planq exists so the package you hand them is clean.

**What about plans that aren't in English?**
Planq reads the drawings, not the language. We've validated against Spanish-labelled plans — "PUERTA 85 cm" is recognized as a door 850 mm wide.

**What file types?**
PDF, PNG, JPG, TIFF, DXF. DWG is proprietary — convert to DXF and we'll read it.

**Whose code does it use?**
Currently the 2020 NBC Alberta Edition. Other jurisdictions on request.

**How accurate is it?**
Honest answer: it catches the obvious stuff reliably and the subtle stuff most of the time. Treat it as a thorough first reviewer, not a substitute for a stamp. The extracted data panel is there so you can verify what it read before trusting what it flagged.

**Can it handle my project size?**
Single-family detached up through mid-rise mixed-use. If your sheet count is in the hundreds, talk to us — we'll set up batch ingestion.

**Does it work on older or hand-drawn plans?**
If the plan is legible in a scan or photo, Planq can extract data from it. Quality of results scales with image quality — a clean PDF will always outperform a blurry photo.

**Is my data secure?**
Plans are stored in encrypted cloud infrastructure and are never shared with third parties. You own your data. We process it to generate your report and nothing else.
